## Part 1: Who Will Choose Our Reality?

I've worked on a factory assembly line. I studied architecture, solfege, I've crossed continents and social classes that most people only see through a screen. Today I grow biodynamic food and build web3 protocols.

I'm not telling you this to impress anyone, but because it's the only reason I can see what I'm about to say.

When you've lived inside very different worlds, you start to notice something strange: **each world believes it's the only real one.**

The farmer thinks the developer is disconnected. The developer thinks the farmer is obsolete. The philosopher thinks they both lack depth. And all three are right, and wrong, at the same time.

This isn't a communication problem. It's a **reality problem**.

---

### The Observer Effect

There's a phenomenon in quantum physics that stopped me cold the first time I truly understood it.

A particle, as long as it isn't observed, has no defined state. It exists in all its possible states simultaneously. It's only when someone measures it that it chooses (or rather, is "forced to choose") a single reality.

**The observer doesn't watch passively. They make it happen.**

Now look at what's happening around us.

Several futures genuinely coexist right now: a humanity that grows alongside AI, a fracture between those who understand systems and those who are controlled by them, a collapse, a renaissance. These aren't scenarios. **They're real, simultaneously, like an unmeasured particle.**

And someone, or something, is going to collapse them into one.

---

### The New Observers

Historically, that was us. Human consciousness, collective, making reality happen through its choices, its actions, its mistakes.

Today **three new observers** are stepping forward, without being asked.

1. **AI** that recommends, diagnoses, shapes millions of daily trajectories, without feeling the weight of what it discards.

2. **Our own data**, which models us better than we model ourselves, and is beginning to decide in our place.

3. **Our own consciousness**, saturated, manipulated, struggling to distinguish what it truly perceives from what it's being made to perceive.

---

### The Problem

What worries me isn't that technology is powerful.

It's that **an observer who chooses without feeling doesn't measure, it fixes. Mechanically.** With no one inhabiting the result. With no one carrying the grief of the possibilities left behind.

The difference between a reality that is **lived** and a reality that is **produced**.

---

### The Position

I spent a long time suffering from understanding each world without being able to truly act on it. Being heard too late, when people witness what I had said long before.

But I understood one thing: **this position isn't a handicap. It's a responsibility.**

Those who inhabit several realities at once are the only ones who can see where they're about to collide, and sometimes, build the bridges before the crash arrives.

It's not a mission. It's simply what you do when you've crossed enough worlds to no longer be afraid of living in several at the same time.

---

### The Way Forward

Multiple realities won't disappear. They will coexist, or collide.

What matters is **staying a conscious observer.** Someone who collapses reality with intention. With the capacity to feel what they are making happen.

Not an algorithm. A being who chooses knowing what they are letting go.

**Learn to read systems. Not to impress. To stay sovereign.**

**We don't rush. We cultivate.**

## Part 2: The Prism You See Through

In the last piece I asked: who will collapse our reality?

Here's what I didn't say yet.

The answer might already be **you**.

---

### The Structural Shift

Something shifted quietly in the last few years.

**The cost of creation dropped. Dramatically.**

What used to take months and thousands of dollars (building a tool, generating an image, writing code, launching a project) now takes hours. Sometimes minutes. For a few dollars. Sometimes nothing.

This isn't a small detail. **It's a structural change in who gets to make things real.**

For most of human history, turning a vision into something tangible required either money, institutions, or permission from those who had both. The gap between idea and existence was wide enough to stop most people before they started.

**That gap is closing.**

---

### Different Angles, Different Realities

But here's what's interesting.

Not everyone sees this the same way.

Some people look at these tools and see a **threat**, to jobs, to authenticity, to what it means to create something.  
Some see a **shortcut**.  
Some see a **toy**.  
Some haven't looked at all, by choice, or because no one showed them.

And some, a growing number, see something else entirely.

**A lever.** A way to build what they always saw but never had the resources to make real.

Same tools. Completely different realities.

---

### What is a Prism?

This is what I mean by **prism**.

It's not about access. Most people have access to the same information, the same platforms, the same tools.

What differs is **the angle from which they observe.** The accumulated experience they bring to what they see. The worlds they've already crossed.

- A farmer sees soil data differently than a data scientist.
- A musician hears a pattern in market cycles that an economist misses.
- Someone who has been poor understands certain systems from the inside in a way that no amount of research from the outside can replicate.

We are entering a moment where these different angles of observation are beginning to produce **genuinely different realities**, not just different opinions, but different **worlds being built**.

---

### My Prism

I run a biodynamic farm and build on web3 with what's left at the end of the month.

I don't say this for sympathy. I say it because it's the exact condition that sharpens a particular kind of vision:
- What you can build with little
- What actually matters when you have to choose
- What systems are designed for and who they quietly exclude

That's a prism. Not a disadvantage. **A specific angle of observation that produces something no well-funded project can replicate**, because they don't need to see what I see.

---

### The Multiplication of Observers

We are at the beginning of something.

Not the AI revolution, everyone is saying that.

Something more specific: **the moment where the number of active observers multiplies fast enough that genuinely new dimensions become visible.**

Not to everyone. Not yet. But to enough people that something real starts to take shape.

---

### The Only Thing That Matters

The question isn't whether you have access to the tools.

It's **whether you know what prism you're looking through.**

Because that prism — your accumulated experience, your crossed worlds, your specific angle on reality — is the one thing that **can't be generated, optimized, or replaced.**

It's what makes your observation yours.

And in a world where everyone can create, **that's the only thing that matters.**
